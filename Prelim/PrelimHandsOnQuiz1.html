Batanes is located in the northernmost part of the Philippines, far from the hustle and bustle of major cities. It is an archipelago consisting of 10 islands, with the main islands being Batan, Sabtang, and Itbayat. It lies in the Luzon Strait, surrounded by the waters of the Philippine Sea to the east and the Bashi Channel to the north, making it the closest Philippine province to Taiwan. Its location offers a stunning mix of rugged coastlines, rolling hills, and traditional Ivatan stone houses, giving it a distinct charm compared to other regions in the country.

Batanes has a rich history that dates back to pre-colonial times, with the Ivatan people being the island's original inhabitants.
